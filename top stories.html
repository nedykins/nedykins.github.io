Income Inequality
At An ALL TIME LOW

New research shows that a shift from defined-benefit retirement plans (pensions) to defined-contribution plans (401(k)s) is aiding income equality and is preparing the majority of Americans for their retirement.

The policy that has been introduced protects and expands Social Security, increasing benefits for millions of Americans. Please, stand with the American people by passing legislation that expands Social Security for current and future generations.

Over the last several decades, employers have moved closer to pension-based retirement plans toward the 401(k) savings model. Participation in these let-us-help-you retirement savings plans have become increasingly equal across income groups because the system is aiming to contribute money to help workers get the most out of retirement and helping them investment smartly. All workers have been getting similar matching contributions from employers and helping them receive many tax benefits, and have been decreasing penalties if they need to tap their savings early.

According to new research by the Economic Policy Institute, nearly nine in 10 families in the top income fifth had retirement account savings, similarly to the eight in 10 families in the bottom income families (see the first figure below).

Additionally, families in the top income fifth accounted for 75 percent of total income, but 88 percent of total savings in retirement accounts, but other income socioeconomic brackets display increased total income and savings for retirement (see the second figure below) illustrating that 401(k)s favor each socioeconomic class.

While disparity across income groups still exists, recent policy changes predicts increased equality among all socioeconomic classes. Displaying retirement security for soon-to-be retirees as well as those who are far from retiring. Policy changes will reduce dependency on seniors relying on Social Security for a majority of their income, and will help people increase investment possibilities.

New policy solutions to the retirement security crisis experienced before will ensure protection for seniors and expand Social Security for millions of Americans.
